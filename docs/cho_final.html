Figure 2. Pooled preoperative hunger ratings (standardized mean differences in VAS or NRS) from randomized trials comparing carbohydrate drinks with fasting in adult surgical patients.

Yagmurdur 2011 and Zhang 2019 standard deviations estimated using p-values. Results from Melis 2006 and Naguib 2001 reported only non-signficant results and could not be included in these meta-analysis. A sensitivity analysis using multiple imputation to include the 2 studies yielded a random effects estimate for the SMD of -0.55 (95% CI, -0.79, -0.30; I2 = 76%). Excluding results from Wendling 2020, SMD = -0.60 (95% CI, -0.90 to -0.31), I2 = 73% (53%, 84%). Some indications of small-study effects in funnel plot, but not supported by Pustejovsky’s test of asymmetry, P = .16.


Figure 3. Pooled hunger ratings (standardized mean differences in VAS or NRS) from randomized trials comparing carbohydrate drinks with clear liquids or water in adult surgical patients.

Tsutsumi 2016 mean estimated from median and range, standard deviation from reported p-value (0.001). Naguib 2001 reported only non-signficant results and could not be included in the meta-analysis; a sensitivity analysis using multiple imputation to add that result yielded a random effects estimate for the SMD of -0.527 (95% CI, -0.51, -0.03; I2 = 51%). Although limited number of studies to assess small-study effects and potential outliers, no indication by Pustejovsky’s test of asymmetry, P = .45.
