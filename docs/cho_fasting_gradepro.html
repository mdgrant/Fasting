Question

Should carbohydrate containing clear liquids (up to 2 hours prior) vs. fasting be used for procedures requiring general anesthesia, regional anesthesia, or procedural sedation and analgesia?

procedures requiring general anesthesia, regional anesthesia, or procedural sedation and analgesia

Desirable Effects

How substantial are the desirable anticipated effects?

Judgement

Research evidence

Additional considerations

Patients given carbohydrate drinks had lower patient-rated hunger or thirst (moderate SOE; level A) and lower rates of hunger or thirst (low SOE; level B-R); differences in patient-rated or rates of preoperative nausea were not apparent (low SOE; level B-R). Patient satisfaction was reported in only two trials and any difference unclear (low SOE; level C-LD).


Undesirable Effects

How substantial are the undesirable anticipated effects?

Judgement

Research evidence

Additional considerations

No aspiration was reported following fasting or with carbohydrate drinks in 29 RCTs and 3 non-randomized studies of interventions (SOE not rated due to lack of events); differences in regurgitation or preoperative vomiting were not apparent (very low and low SOE; levels C-LD and B-R). A meaningful difference was not evident in either residual gastric volume or gastric pH following fasting or carbohydrate drinks (moderate SOE; levels A and B-R).
